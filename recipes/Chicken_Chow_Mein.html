Chicken Chow Mein

Chow Mein, the classic stir-fried noodles is definitely one of the most popular Chinese dishes in and outside Chine

Nutrition:

CALORIES: 632KCAL, CARBOHYDRATES: 61G, PROTEIN: 34G, FAT: 27G, SATURATED FAT: 5G CHOLESTEROL: 111MG, SODIUM: 3187MG, POTASSIUM: 850MG, FIBER: 6G, SUGAR: 5G, VITAMIN A: 9575IU, VITAMIN C: 96.3MG, CALCIUM: 253MG, IRON: 5.7MG


Ingredients:
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 2 cloves garlic - minced
  • 10 ounces Chow Mein noodles (Chinese egg noodles) - (uncooked)
  • 1 big carrot - julienned
  • 2 stalks green onions - cut into halves and then into 2 inch long slices
  • 1 bunch Pak choi - cut diagonally (1-2cm thick)

Chicken Marinade:

  • 1 pound chicken thigh - deboned and cut into bite sizes
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 teaspoon five-spice powder
  • â…› teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1 tablespoon hoisin sauce

Sauce:

  • 5 tablespoons water
  • 4 tablespoons oyster sauce
  • 5 tablespoons soy sauce

Steps:
  1. In a bowl, mix together all the ingredients for the chicken marinade. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and let it marinate for 15-30 minutes.
  2. Meanwhile, cook noodles in water as per package instructions but reduce cooking time by 1 minute. Transfer to a colander and rinse the noodles with cold water to avoid from cooking further and sticking together. Drain well and set aside.
  3. In a small bowl, combine all ingredients for the sauce.
  4. Heat oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat. Fry the marinated chicken by spreading them in one layer and cooking each side for 3 minutes or until golden. Remove chicken from oil and set aside.
  5. In the same wok with oil, add the garlic and cook for a minute or until limp. Turn heat to high and add the noodles and carrots. Stir fry for a minute or two until carrots starts to become tender-crisp.
  6. Pour in the sauce and toss to coat noodles. Add the pak choi, green onions, and the chicken. Mix and cook for another minute.
  7. Transfer to a serving dish and serve.
